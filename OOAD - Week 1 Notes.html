Week 1 Notes: OOAD

OOAD Overview

This class covers building a system or building software through the following phases:

  • Analysis
  • Design
  • Development
  • Implementation

What is a Software?

Fundamental Process: Input (I/P) → Process → Output (O/P)

Types of Software

1. Personal or Small Group Software

  • Limited Set of Behaviours
  • Not very complex
  • Maintained & used by the same group of people
  • Tends to have a short life span

2. Industrial Software

Characterized by high integrity and investment of millions of dollars.

Examples:

  • Banking Systems
  • AutoCAD
  • Aircraft Control Systems
  • Stock Exchange Software
  • Firewalls

Characteristics:

  • Very rich set of behaviours
  • Real-time processing (e.g., When a car brake is pressed, the software is what allows the action)
  • Maintains the integrity of millions of records while allowing concurrent access
  • Command & Control of real-world entities (e.g., train routing, rail systems)

Why Software Projects Fail?

Functional Reasons:

  • Customers learn from the solution (and their requirements change)
  • Business environments and conditions change
  • Business processes are re-engineered
